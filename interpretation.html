Sources & Interpretation

Treaty with the Cherokee, 1798

This treaty pressured the Cherokee to cede land and accept boundaries chosen by the U.S. government.

Indian Removal Act (1830)

The Act claimed removal would be voluntary and beneficial, but in reality gave the President broad authority.

Andrew Jackson’s Message to Congress (1830)

Jackson described removal as kind and protective, portraying Native nations as uncivilized.

Family Stories from the Trail of Tears

Oral histories describe Cherokee families being forced from their homes, suffering hunger and disease.
